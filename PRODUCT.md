# Product

## Register

product

## Users

HMD Secure's 10-20 person commercial team uses this internal CRM throughout
the workday:

- Sales Representatives manage accounts, deals, forecasts, offers, discounts,
  and follow-ups.
- Technical Account Managers manage service cases, escalations, SLAs, and
  customer history.
- Sales Managers inspect pipeline health, stalled deals, targets, assignments,
  and approvals.
- Finance reviews weighted, time-phased forecasts, maintains pricing, exports
  data, and gives final discount approval.

They often need an accurate answer while preparing for a call, updating a deal
after a meeting, resolving a service issue, or answering a forecast question
under time pressure. A new user must complete the core demo workflow without
training.

## Product Purpose

Turn scattered email, personal notes, and spreadsheets into a trustworthy shared
operating picture. Keep account, deal, case, offer, forecast, and activity
history connected; reduce data-entry friction; preserve institutional memory;
and make the next useful action obvious.

Success means Sales and TAMs work from the same customer history, managers can
inspect pipeline health without verbal status collection, and Finance can see
near-term and three-year revenue phasing without requesting a spreadsheet.

## Brand Personality

**Assured, operational, human.**

The product should evoke control, readiness, and trust without feeling
militarized, paranoid, or sterile. Its voice is concise, plain-spoken, and
specific. Prefer "3 deals need attention" over abstract enterprise language.
AI sounds like a capable analyst: factual, editable, and transparent about
uncertainty.

## Reference Products

- **Attio for CRM structure:** borrow its object/list/view mental model,
  spreadsheet-speed inline editing, configurable record pages, saved filtered
  views, table/kanban parity, quick actions, and workflows embedded in the
  system of record.
- **Clay for AI-assisted data work:** borrow its visible input-to-output
  pipeline, column-level enrichment, batch actions, per-row execution state,
  source provenance, retry/review controls, and the sense that AI is a tool
  operating on structured data rather than a detached chatbot.
- **HMD Secure for identity and tone:** retain the industrial palette, direct
  language, operational seriousness, and restrained use of accent.

The target is not a visual mash-up. It is Attio's CRM clarity plus Clay's
reviewable AI mechanics, expressed through HMD Secure's identity and the
specific device, service, case, approval, and forecast rules of this product.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS dashboards composed entirely of soft cards.
- Cyan-on-navy or purple-blue AI gradients, glow, glassmorphism, and decorative
  neural imagery.
- Consumer-phone marketing layouts transplanted directly into product UI.
- Dense legacy CRM tables with no prioritization or next-action guidance.
- Tactical cosplay: camouflage, faux terminal styling, excessive warning
  colors, or military iconography.
- AI assistants presented as magical, autonomous, or more certain than the
  underlying data.
- Any presentation that implies this hackathon prototype is an official HMD
  production service.
- A pixel-for-pixel Attio or Clay clone, including their branding, proprietary
  assets, exact layouts, copy, icons, or distinctive ornamental treatments.

## Design Principles

1. **Signal before decoration.** Prominent color, badges, charts, and motion
   identify priority, state, ownership, risk, or required action.
2. **One shared operating picture.** Account, deal, case, offer, and timeline
   context remain visibly connected.
3. **Dense, not cramped.** Efficient tables and compact typography retain clear
   grouping, alignment, and hierarchy.
4. **AI with receipts.** Suggestions show the customer signal or record that
   motivated them, remain editable, and never silently mutate business data.
5. **Forecasts are temporal.** Device and service revenue remain distinct, with
   near-term and three-year phasing visible rather than flattened into one
   number.
6. **Urgency has a budget.** Accent and semantic colors are rare enough to
   preserve meaning.
7. **Fast paths for real work.** Common updates require minimal navigation,
   preserve context, and provide immediate feedback.
8. **Structured AI, not chat-first AI.** Put generated values, summaries,
   classifications, and recommendations beside the records they affect, with
   visible inputs, sources, status, cost or scope where relevant, and an
   explicit review step before consequential changes.
9. **Views are working lenses.** Let each role save filters, columns, sorting,
   grouping, and table/kanban presentation without duplicating underlying
   account, deal, or case data.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Meet WCAG 2.2 AA. Provide visible keyboard focus and keyboard-complete
workflows, never rely on color alone, use accessible data-table structures,
maintain practical target sizes, support common forms of color-vision
deficiency, and honor `prefers-reduced-motion`.
